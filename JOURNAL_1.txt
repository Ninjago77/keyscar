Now, the first thing I researched was the chip:

1. I needed to control 2 usb male modules and these where the choices that came up:-

- RP2040 (the pico chip, very nice and easy to use)
- ATmega32U4 (what the other hackyduckys use)
- WCH CH552G (really small but cheap and powerful)
- ESP32-S2 (has wifi)

Out of all of these, all of them had problems

- RP2040 is 1$ and uses 3v3 which means i had to deal with safeguarding both usb module's 5v as well as convert its output
- ATmega32U4 is 4$??????? way too expensive
- ESP32-S2 is 2$ and also 3v3 (idont want to deal w/that), and has wifi (which is good but I want a challenge so no wifi)

And the one I chose is:
- WCH CH552G is 25 cents, has a built in usb controller, builtin stored memory, uart, spi, all for litterally 25 cents.

BUT, with something this goated always comes a caveat. it only has 1 usb controller.

which is A-okay bcs im just going to slap on 2 of em (they are 25 cents, so cheap, so 2 of em is better than using any other chip)

2. BUT, with 2 chips comes, communication choices, either:

 - spi : (more speed, more data)
 - uart : (easy to use, 1-2Mbps cap)

I chose uart for now, bcs this chip isnt going to be as easy to program as a pico, which means the communication protocols might be way too advanced, so I will choose the easier one for now: uart.
